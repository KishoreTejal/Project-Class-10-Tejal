Languages In Madhya Pradesh


Madhya Pradesh, often referred to as the “Heart of India,” is home to a variety of languages reflecting its cultural diversity. The primary languages spoken in Madhya Pradesh include:

Hindi: The official language, widely spoken across the state.

Urdu: Predominantly spoken in Bhopal, Kurwai, and Burhanpur, especially in communities with a significant Muslim population.

Marathi: Spoken in regions bordering Maharashtra.

Malvi: Common in the Malwa region.

Nimadi: Spoken in the Nimar region.

Bundeli: Used in the Bundelkhand region.

Bagheli: Predominantly spoken in the Bagelkhand region.

Gondi: A tribal language spoken by the Gondi people.

Bhili: Another tribal language.

Korku: Spoken by the Korku tribe.

These languages and dialects contribute to the rich linguistic tapestry of Madhya Pradesh, making it a culturally vibrant state



Languages In Bihar


Bihar is home to a rich tapestry of languages, reflecting its diverse cultural heritage. The primary languages spoken in Bihar include:

Hindi: The official language of the state.

Urdu: Recognized as a second official language in 15 districts.

Bhojpuri: Widely spoken in the western parts of Bihar.

Maithili: Predominantly spoken in the northern regions and has official recognition under the Eighth Schedule to the Constitution of India.

Magahi: Commonly spoken in the central and southern parts of Bihar, including around the capital, Patna.

Angika: Spoken in the southeastern parts of Bihar.

Vajjika (Bajjika): A dialect of Maithili, spoken in the Vaishali region.

Additionally, there are other languages and dialects such as Sadri, Surjapuri, Bengali, and various tribal languages like Kurukh, Santali, and Munda.
